About

My name is Priyankari (Priya) Perali. I am a masters student in computer science at Texas A&M University, advised by Dr. Robin Murphy. My master's research is in Human-Robot Interaction, specifically investigating the role of touch interaction between trapped victims in rubble and small unmanned ground vehicles (sUGVs). Besides my thesis work, I work in curating disaster datasets of aerial imagery and developing machine learning/computer vision models for damage assessments, funded by the AI Institute for Societal Decision Making. (Check out the CRASAR-U-DRIODs dataset, if interested!). My research interests are in human-robot interaction, machine learning, and humanitarian engineering.

Previously, I graduated from The Ohio State University for my bachelors, majoring in computer science and engineering. At Ohio State, I was a part of the Humanitarian Engineering Scholars program, where I worked on designing engineering solutions for marginalized and underserved communities.
